Two way ANOVA

The two-way ANOVA is an extension of one-way ANOVA that introduces two factors. By Two-way ANOVA we are able to know

  • whether the means of the population corresponding to factors are different 
  • whether the means corresponding to one factor are different to another factor
  • whether there is any interaction between the factors or not

The effect of one independent variable on the dependent variable averaged across the levels of the other independent variable is known as the main effect. Similar to one-way ANOVA, we need to perform F-test to determine if the means are different.

We can also plot the cell means of factors to see whether there is an interaction between the factors.

Chi-Square

Contingency tables are useful in situations involving multiple population proportions and are used to classify sample observations according to two or more characteristics. The Chi-Square test is used to determine the statistically significant difference among multiple population proportions.
