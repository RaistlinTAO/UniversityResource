Most business decision making is about either capitalizing on a business opportunity or addressing a business problem. Often business problems are not obvious. Most times, we see the symptoms or the signals rather than the underlying problem. So, in topic one, we start our journey with an introduction to business research and try to figure out the most appropriate type of research for the problem at hand.

The appropriate type of research depends on the level of uncertainty associated with the situation. For example, we would use causal research where we have a very clear understanding of all the factors affecting the business problem. On the other extreme, we use exploratory research where things are not so clear, and we need to make sense of what is happening.

Business research plays an important part in a data-driven decision-making process. It allows decision-makers to consider carefully planned, evidence-based decisions rather than relying on their gut feeling. In this topic, we explore how the decisions are linked to research objectives, then translated into research hypotheses.

Upon completion of this topic, you should be able to do the following:

  • define business/data analytics and understand its role in the business decision-making framework
  • know the major phases of the business problem solving / research process
  • understand problem definition process
  • understand research design methods
  • describe the main data types and sampling methods

We also revisit several fundamental statistical concepts in preparation for the next topic.
