Previously we discussed the link between business decisions and research objectives and how they are translated into testable propositions. This week we focus on 'hypothesis testing'. A formal procedure for seeing enough 'statistical' evidence to support a particular claim or a proposition.

In your foundation studies, we covered a variety of commonly used hypothesis testing procedures that relate to a single population. This week, we extend the same procedure to compare population parameters or samples statistics drawn from two populations. For example, is the average electricity price in Melbourne different from the average electricity price in Sydney? Another example, is there a greater proportion of young males exceeding speed limits on a regular basis?

This topic is a collection of statistical tests covering many different instances of comparing two populations. In class, we discuss how to select the test most appropriate for a given set of conditions and critically investigate the validity of the assumptions underlying each hypothesis testing procedure.

At the completion of this topic, you will be able to:

  • setup the null and alternative hypotheses to perform statistical hypothesis testing for a difference
  • risk or errors associated with the statistical hypothesis test
  • formulate decision rule in a statistical hypothesis test
  • use the test statistic, critical value and p-value in taking the decision in a statistical hypothesis test
  • conduct statistical hypothesis tests in Excel
