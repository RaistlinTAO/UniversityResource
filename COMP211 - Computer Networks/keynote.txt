Hosts listen to the channel before transmitting.
If the channel is busy, hosts wait for a random backoff time before retrying.
With low p, collisions are less likely, and the random backoff mechanism helps avoid collisions.
2 high p
Divides the channel into time slots, and each host gets a specific time slot to transmit.
Efficient when the number of hosts is fixed and known, and the probability of collision is high.
